About

I didn't take the straight road to this chair — thank goodness.

Before I was a therapist, I was a sex educator. I spent years talking openly, without flinching, about bodies and desire and shame. What I kept noticing was that so many people believed something was wrong with them, when really they'd just never been in a room that got them. That's why I became a therapist — and it's the feeling I try to recreate every time someone sits down across from me.

In the room, I'm warm and pretty laid-back. I'll laugh with you, go deep when it's time, and know when to come up for air. I believe healing is allowed to have color and joy and a little whimsy in it — that your inner child gets a seat at the table, and that rest is a right, not a reward.

And, because it matters: I'm a therapist who has a therapist. I'd never ask you to go somewhere I'm not willing to go myself.

I'm not here to tell you you're "so strong."

You've heard it your whole life — you're so resilient, you handle everything so well, I don't know how you do it. And you're tired, because the people saying it have no idea what it costs you to keep doing it.

Here's what I actually believe: your people survived things that should have been unsurvivable — and not so you could spend this one life white-knuckling your way through, calling it "fine." You're allowed to want more than fine. That's the whole reason I do this.

Fully credentialed. Completely un-precious about it.

I've got the training and the framed pieces of paper to prove it — and I'm also not the kind of therapist who treats the medical model like scripture. I don't think a diagnosis is the most interesting thing about you, and I don't believe healing only happens in a tidy 50-minute box.

There's no single right way to come home to yourself. So we'll find yours — practical and intuitive, structured and a little magic, whatever it takes. Sometimes that's skills and boundaries and nervous-system stuff. Sometimes it's pulling out every tool in the kit. Always, it means you're the expert on you, and I'm just genuinely, unshakably in your corner.

I hold an MA in counseling and am a National Certified Counselor (NCC). I see clients by telehealth across Georgia and in person in Atlanta. A Good Faith Estimate is available on request.

Who I work with

Chances are, you're exactly who I'm here for.

I work with individuals and couples — and yes, relationships with more than two people, too. Most folks find me for one of these reasons, though plenty are a beautiful mix of several.

Eldest daughters

The one who holds everyone

You were the responsible one, the fixer, the family's unofficial therapist before you could legally drive. Let's let you be a person with needs — not just a role everyone relies on. Rest included.

Complex relationships

Relationships that don't fit the template

Blended, long-distance, on-again, hard to summarize at Thanksgiving — we'll untangle it without anyone telling you what your relationship is "supposed" to look like.

Sexuality

Curious, shifting, surprising desire

Maybe your relationship with desire has gone quiet, gotten louder, or surprised you. Nothing about it is a problem to solve. We'll get curious together — gently, shame-free.

LGBTQIA+

Affirming, full stop

Your identity isn't the thing we're here to fix or examine — it's just part of the whole, wonderful person I get to work with. You won't have to educate me to be understood.

Poly & nonmonogamy

No pathologizing your structure

I won't treat jealousy like proof something's broken. I know the language, I respect the work it takes, and we can focus on the real stuff: communication, agreements, and the very human feelings underneath.

Kink community

You're safe here

Your kink is not a symptom, and I won't treat it like a wound that needs explaining. I understand consent, negotiation, and aftercare as the skilled, intimate things they are.

And if none of those fit perfectly? That's okay too. You don't have to come with a category. You just have to come as you are.
